Corpus Analysis

For my corpus analysis, I used Voyant Tools to explore word frequency patterns in the text. The Cirrus word cloud shows which words appear the most and gives insight into what characters and themes dominate the writing.

Larger words like carlos, almeyda, pacomo, and eugenia indicate the main characters. Smaller words like “sir,” “shall,” and “enter” show the dramatic, play-like structure of the text. This visualization helps reveal tone, character importance, and the emotional intensity of the story.

This line graph complements the word cloud by showing how specific terms—like carlos, almeyda, pacomo, count, and car—fluctuate in frequency across different segments of the text. While the word cloud gives a snapshot of overall term prominence, the line graph reveals narrative dynamics and character focus over time. For example, the term count peaks in segment 3, suggesting a moment of emphasis or introduction. In contrast, carlos and almeyda show steadier presence, indicating their sustained importance throughout the story. Together, these visualizations offer both macro and micro insights into the text's structure and themes.
